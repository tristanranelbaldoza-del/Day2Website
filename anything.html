A paper origami bird, turning slowly in the afternoon light of an old garden. There is no script. There are no children on camera. We made this film the way we want the world to meet our children — with care, with warmth, without spectacle.

We don't run orphanages.
We end them.

The research is settled: institutions harm children, even the well-run ones. For ten years we've done one thing — find a family for every child in our care, and fund that family until the child is a working adult. That's it. That's the whole model.

01

Find a family

We partner with community leaders, faith groups, and relatives to place each child in a home that already loves them — or is ready to.

02

Fund a childhood

Twelve years of school fees, uniforms, books, meals, medical care, and a monthly stipend to the caregiver. Every child, every month.

03

Walk the road

A local social worker visits every family monthly. They know each child's name, their grades, their dog, their fears. No algorithm.

The cheapest thing we ever did was stop building orphanages. The most expensive — and the most worth it — was committing to pay for one childhood at a time, for as long as it takes.
— Amara Okafor, Founder
