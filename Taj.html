Agra, India · 27°10′30″N 78°02′31″E ● Built 1632 – 1653 A tomb for Mumtaz

A drawing becomes a tomb on the Yamuna.

Shah Jahan commissioned the Taj Mahal as a mausoleum for his wife Mumtaz Mahal in 1632. It took twenty thousand artisans, one thousand elephants, and twenty-one years to complete. Scroll — the elevation drawing resolves into the finished marble on the river.

A mausoleum for a wife,
and a grief.

Built in white Makrana marble, inlaid with twenty-eight kinds of precious and semi-precious stones, the Taj Mahal is widely considered the jewel of Mughal architecture and the finest expression of love built in stone.

01 · Began 1632

Construction began under Shah Jahan as a tomb for his wife Mumtaz Mahal.

02 · Main dome 73m

Height of the central onion dome, flanked by four slender minarets.

03 · Workers 20k

Artisans, masons, and craftsmen. Plus a thousand elephants hauling stone.

04 · Finished 1653

Twenty-one years after it was begun. A UNESCO World Heritage Site since 1983.
